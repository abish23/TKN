About Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is one of India's most ancient classical dance forms, originating in the temples of Tamil Nadu. It is an art of devotion — a blend of rhythm, emotion, and storytelling that unites body, mind, and spirit.

History

Bharatanatyam has its roots in the Natya Shastra, a 2000-year-old text by Sage Bharata Muni. Originally performed as temple offerings by Devadasis, it evolved into a globally respected art form — preserving sacred traditions through movement.

Key Elements of Bharatanatyam

Each Bharatanatyam performance combines rhythm, storytelling, and divine expression.

Nrtta - Pure dance movements focusing on rhythm and geometry.

Nritya - Expressive storytelling through facial expressions and gestures.

Natya - The dramatic aspect that brings mythological stories to life.

Musical Instruments

Music forms the heartbeat of Bharatanatyam. These classical instruments accompany the dancer and bring life to each rhythm:

Mridangam – Main percussion instrument providing rhythm.

Nattuvangam – Cymbals played by the guru to guide the tempo.

Veena – String instrument symbolizing the goddess Saraswati.

Flute – Brings melody and emotional depth.
